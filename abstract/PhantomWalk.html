Phantom Walk: Reducing the Post-Migration Translation Path via Proactive Translation Dissemination in Multi-GPU Systems

Jihun Choi, Jaesang Moh and Tae Hee Han

26 July 2026

Abstract

Multi-GPU systems rely on unified virtual memory to provide a global address space, which inevitably triggers page migrations. Our analysis shows that reducing migrations further is difficult even with advanced policies. These migrations invalidate page ta- ble entries (PTEs) across GPUs and generate migration-induced misses (M-misses), which constitute a large portion of all translation requests and significantly degrade performance. This paper intro- duces Phantom Walk, which accelerates M-miss address translation by reducing GPU-local page faults and page table walks through a shorter post-migration translation path. Our evaluation shows that Phantom Walk improves overall performance by 44.37% on average.
