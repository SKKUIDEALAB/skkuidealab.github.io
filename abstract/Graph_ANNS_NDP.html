Efficient Graph Index Updates for ANNS with Near-Data Processing

Sung Yong Yoo, Hyeong Jin Kim, Tae Hee Han

15 October 2025

Abstract

Dynamic graph indexing in real-time graph databases is typically performed through either periodic rebuilding or update-based methods. While rebuilding preserves graph quality, it becomes increasingly expensive as the dataset grows, leading to higher indexing time and hardware costs. Update-based methods, by comparison, are more efficient and cost-effective, with a potential trade-off in graph quality. This work proposes an update-based indexing approach that integrates algorithmic optimization with Near-data processing (NDP) to reduce overhead while maintaining graph quality. Our method reduces end-to-end update latency by up to 75.8%, with consistently improved performance across diverse datasets and update ratios, making it well-suited for dynamic, large-scale approximate nearest neighbor search (ANNS) workloads.
